This guide will walk you through everything from setting up your classes to analyzing individual student performance.

1. Getting Started: Importing Data

The dashboard relies on data you import. There are two types of imports: Rosters (Who is in the class) and Class Data (How they are doing).

Step A: The Roster Import

Before you can track progress, the system needs to know who belongs in your classroom.

  1. Click the Import button in the top header.
  2. Select "Import Roster".
  3. Choose your roster file (Excel/CSV).
  4. What happens next?
    • The system checks your file against the current dashboard.
    • Strict Mode: If a student is in your file, they are added. If a student is not in your file, they are removed from the dashboard. This ensures your dashboard exactly matches your official list.

Step B: Importing Student Progress

Once your students are seated, you can import their work.

  1. Click the Import button.
  2. Select "Import Class Data".
  3. You can select one file or a whole batch of files (e.g., all 85 files for Science).
  4. The Import Summary:
    • After processing, a summary window will appear.
    • Successes: Shows how many students were updated.
    • Issues: If a student's name in the file essentially matches someone on your roster (e.g., "Jon Smith" vs "Jonathan Smith"), the system might ask you to confirm.
    • Results: Your students' cards will update instantly with their latest grades.

2. Managing Your Classroom Layout

You can arrange the student cards to match your physical classroom seating.

  • Move a Student: Simply click and drag any student card to a new spot.
  • Move Between Subjects: Drag a student from the "Science" quadrant to "Math" to transfer them relative to your dashboard view.
  • Save Your Seating Chart:
    1. Hover over the label of any room (e.g., "SCIENCE HR").
    2. A small menu will appear. Click "Save Layout".
    3. If you ever mess up the cards, just click "Restore Layout" to snap them back to your saved arrangement.

3. Monitoring Class Progress

Using the Class History View

To see a high-level overview of everyone's work:

  1. Click the "Class History" button in the top header.
  2. This opens a detailed list view.
  3. By Student: See a list of students and scan for missing assignments or low grades.
  4. By Class: Group everyone by subject to see if a specific class is struggling overall.
  5. Filter Issues: Toggle the "Show Issues Only" button to hide students who are on track, letting you focus solely on those who need help.

Filtering the Dashboard (Student List & Filters)

You can refine the main dashboard to find specific groups of students.

  1. Click the Filter Icon (funnel) in any room header.
  2. Sort: Arrange students by Name, Grade, or Progress.
  3. Filter:
    • Time Window: Pick a Start and End date to see only work done this week/month.
    • Alerts: Show only students with "Low Progress" or "Missing Assignments".
  4. Live Preview: As you change settings, the list on the right updates instantly to show you exactly who matches your criteria.

4. Deep Dive: Student Progress Detail

Click on any Student Card to open their Progress Detail View. This is your command center for an individual student.

The Header

  • Student Selector: Quickly switch to another student using the dropdown on the left.
  • Class Selector: The colored dropdown shows the current class. If a student is taking two science classes (e.g., Biology AND Physics), use this dropdown to switch between them.
  • Export: Click "Export" to save an image of this report to share with parents or admin.

The Semester Calendar

  • This shows a 4-month view of activity.
  • Color Intensity: Days with more activity appear darker.
  • Interactive: Click any day to see a pop-up list of exactly what assignments were submitted on that date.

The Performance Graph

  • Left Axis (Bars): The number of activities completed each week.
  • Right Axis (Lines):
    • Solid Green Line: The student's Completion % (How close they are to finishing the course).
    • Dashed White Line: The student's Average Grade.
  • Hover: Hover over any dot to see the exact numbers for that week.

With these tools, you can move from a "Big Picture" view of your entire roster down to the specific assignment details of a single student in seconds.
